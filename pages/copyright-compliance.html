Everyone uploading material to Learning Space must ensure that they are legally entitled to do so.

All materials on Learning Space must be regarded as public documents. This is partly because any document may have to be made accessible via a Freedom of Information request, but more importantly because any student or staff member might download it and use it in another context, and lead to ‘downstream’ claims against the University.

When using material from the web, always check the website’s Terms of Use, which can usually be found at the foot of the home page.

Many will state ‘copyright reserved’ or similar. This does not prohibit use, as small amounts may be used under what is termed ‘fair use’, ‘fair dealing’ or ‘fair academic use.’ It is legally acceptable to use small amounts of other people’s work without the need to seek copyright permission. There is greater latitude in the case of academic use. The essentials in the latter case are that it must be legitimate academic work and not for profit, that the amount must not be excessive, and that the source and copyright should be acknowledged wherever possible. As there are not absolute rules, colleagues need to consider carefully what constitutes an acceptable amount, and what constitutes appropriate acknowledgement.

In brief, the current legislation allows copying of works in any medium provided that:

  • it is used solely to illustrate a point
  • it is not for commercial purposes and does not compromise the owner’s ability to benefit from the work
  • The amount used must be small and meet ‘fair dealing’ criteria
  • it must be acknowledged appropriately

Other material may be stated to be ‘royalty free’ or be available under a ‘creative commons’ licence. Whilst these allow re-use without payment, both explicitly create an obligation to acknowledge the source, and may place limitations on the extent to which materials may be modified or used for differing purposes.

Students uploading work for assessment to Learning Space must also ensure that they comply with the University’s Academic Integrity Policy.

Staff must also be aware of the conditions of the Academic Integrity Policy.
